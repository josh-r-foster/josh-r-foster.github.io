Thaler's Save More Tomorrow (SMarT) Plan

  1. Found a company wanting to improve employee savings.
  2. Thaler worked with those empolyees who refused traditional financial advisement.
  3. "Would you like to save more with your next raise?"
    • Amount was less than their raise.
    • Continued with each raise.

Ultimately saved more than those with financial advisor.

Why did this plan work?

  1. Present bias: it sidesteps short-run impatience.
  2. Loss aversion: it didn't require a decrease in consumption.
  3. Default effect: a small cost to switching kept employees saving.
