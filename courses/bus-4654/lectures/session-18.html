Easterlin Paradox.

Happiness varies by relative income, but not by absolute income.

  • Being richer than your neighbor will make you happier than your neighbor.
  • Being richer in five years will not make you happier than you are now.

  • Business.
  • Non-profit.

In these roles we've explored several systematic errors and biases in judgment.

  • Reference-dependent preferences.
  • Short-term impatience.
  • Subjective probabilities.
  • Motivated reasoning.

  • Warm glow giving.

On a personal note, some things behavioural economics has taught me.

1) Avoid comparisons.

Reference points and loss aversion destroy our subjective appreciation of every-day pleasantries.

2) Be honest about procrastination.

When important things in life get hard, never expect your future self to be stronger or more prepared to deal with them than you are today.

3) Recognize that your preferences can change.

The decisions we make today shape the opportunities we give ourselves tomorrow. Be mindful that your future self may want very different things from what you want today.
