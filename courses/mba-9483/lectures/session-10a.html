Easterlin Paradox.

Happiness varies by relative income, but not by absolute income.

  • Being richer than your neighbor will make you happier than your neighbor.
  • Being richer in five years will not make you happier than you are now.

Why does this happen?

Hedonic adaptation.

After a positive (or negative) event, and a corresponding increase (decrease) in utility, people return to a relatively stable baseline affect.
