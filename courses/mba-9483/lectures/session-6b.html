Key takeaways.

  1. Principal-agent problems involve mis-aligned incentives between the owners of a firm and those hired to act on their behalf. These problems are exacerbated by the presence of asymmetric information.
  2. Safelite considered lowering their installers' guaranteed pay as a screening mechanism to avoid adverse selection.
  3. Safelite considered raising their installers' maximum pay to align their incentives with the company's, which would avoid moral hazard.
